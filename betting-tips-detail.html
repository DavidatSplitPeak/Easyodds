Thursday’s horse racing tips are brought to you by the returning Paul Jacobs, who is filling in for Jake Johns all this week! After being in the winners yesterday, can the 5x NAPS champion find more profit for the Easyodds readers on Thursday with his expert horse racing tips?

Doncaaster Tips

4.50 – Doncaster – This looks a fair staying event for the grade, but there can be no getting away from the claims of DEW POND as he has only gone up 6lbs for his two victories and looked a long way in front of the handicapper when easing to victory over course and distance last time out.

Of course this is a going to be a much sterner test for the son of Motivator, but the way he cleared away in the closing stages makes this progressive four-year-old one to stay on the right side of until he is beaten.

Of his rivals, Hurry Home Papa won nicely last time out, but this mark has proved a no go zone before so a bigger danger could come from Rock On Bollinski. The last named wasn’t suited by the way the race was run last time out and remains in good form.

BET NOW – DEW POND

Yarmouth Tips

5.35 – Yarmouth – This sprint finale shouldn’t really take an awful lot of winning and the drop to the minimum trip for the first time in his career could well be the making of the Paco Boy filly GABRIELLE.

She has travelled through her three races to date as though she could be an 85 plus rated horse and showed particular promise on rain softened ground when fifth of 10 behind Jadaayil at Newbury, not for the first time fading in the closing stages.

She failed to sparkle on fast ground last time out and this surface allied to as fair mark makes her the value bet over the likes of top weight Olympic Runner and course and distance winner Shypen.

BET NOW – GABRIELLE

Newcastle Tips

6.45 – Newcastle – Where Orion’s Bow has found his recent improvement from is anyone’s guess, but as his trainer Dandy Nicholls said when they are rolling like he is you just have to keep on running them and his easy win at Thirsk off a mark of 85 had to be seen to be believed.

Now up to a mark of 91 in a better race, the handicapper could conceivably have given him more weight, he has to be involved if in the same sort of form.

However, he will hopefully make the market for us and I will be having a lumpy each-way wager on PATRICK. He never showed at York last time out (Bogart ahead of him), but returned to an artificial surface will be a big advantage to him and although a mark of 92 is no giveaway, he remains capable of winning off this mark.

BET NOW – PATRICK
